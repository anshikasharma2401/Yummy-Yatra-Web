Description:

Rasgulla is a popular Bengali sweet made from chenna (cottage cheese) balls cooked in light sugar syrup. It is soft, spongy, and soaked in syrup, making it a refreshing dessert.

Prep Time:

‚è±Ô∏è 60 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar (to curdle the milk)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 cardamom pods (optional)
  • Rose water or kewra water (optional, few drops)

Instructions:

  1. Boil milk and curdle it using lemon juice or vinegar.
  2. Strain using muslin cloth and wash under water to remove sourness.
  3. Hang the chenna for 30 mins to drain excess water.
  4. Knead chenna until smooth and soft. Make small smooth balls.
  5. Boil water with sugar in a wide pan. Add cardamom if using.
  6. Gently add rasgullas into the boiling syrup. Cover and cook for 15‚Äì20 minutes.
  7. Let them cool. Add rose water if desired. Chill before serving.
